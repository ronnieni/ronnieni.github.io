Do you have plants at home?

That's great! Home grown foods are not only more organic, but you can monitor water and chemical use as well while also maintaining the amount of plants on the planet. Keep it up!
Great start! Try growing some of your own foods, or researching what kinds of plants are native to your environment.

When purchasing new appliances such as a refrigerator, do you usually look for the Energy Star label to save energy?

Awesome! Not only are you saving money, but you're also saving a lot of energy!
Make sure that you check for EnergyStar labels before buying new appliances - it will save you a lot in the future! EnergyStar labeled products use the least possible energy to fulfill a task, so it will not only save money, but also power!

Is your house insulated?

That's great! Stay sustainable~!
It may be more expensive, but insulating your house will help conserve energy by blocking warm/cool air inside. Try looking into insulation methods!
There are many ways to insulate your house - this includes double paning your windows, green-roofing your house, or even just blocking gaps and cracks in your walls with old blankets. Check around the house to see what kind of insulation you may have!
